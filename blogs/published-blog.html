How Do I Increase My Optimum Internet Speed?

The speed test is only a rough estimate of your Optimum Internet speed at any given time. In reality, plenty of factors affect internet speed. Both active and passive actions drag your internet speed down. Here is a quick look at some of the factors that can help get your internet speeds back up to the baseline.

  • Pause or close unused web pages: If any pages are open on your browser but not being used, they still use up bandwidth and take up a part of your internet speed. Hence, if you want to get full use of your Optimum Internet plan and speed, it would be wise to close any web pages or buffering websites.
  • Get a wired connection: It is no secret that the internet that runs over an Ethernet cable is much faster than Wi-Fi. Of course, recent evolution in Wi-Fi technologies has made the difference between the two quite smaller, but cabled internet connections are typically faster.
  • Reset your modem & router: A buildup of cache and other temporary files in your internet equipment can also slow down your internet speeds. If you find your connection slowing down a little too much, it might be time to reset or reboot your equipment for a fresh start.
  • Disconnect idling devices: If you use a wireless network, too many devices connected to it can lead to slower internet speeds. Hence, if there are any devices such as smartphones, tablets, laptops, etc., idling on your network, disconnect them and try checking the speed again. It will probably get better.
  • Clear your browser cache: A buildup of cache or other files on your browser history may also lead to a slower internet speed on your laptop. To combat this, simply clear out your browser cache and restart your computer to fix internet speeds.

If you have tried all of this and your Optimum Internet speed does not improve, it may be because your need and usage have climbed up. In such cases, you can consider getting an Optimum plan upgrade for your home and help everyone get the internet speeds they need for their activities.

How Much Internet Speed Do I Need?

The internet speed you need depends on the activity you usually do online. Smaller families of 2 to 3 people can typically do all they want with lower speeds, ranging between 25 Mbps to 100 Mbps. For larger families or people with heavier internet use such as streaming, video conferencing, or more, higher internet speeds of up to 500 Mbps would be more fitting.

For people who are into competitive gaming or streaming a lot of online content such as Netflix, speeds up to 1000 Mbps would be the best choice. As your internet usage evolves and grows, so might your ultimate internet needs.
